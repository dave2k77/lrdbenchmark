# Author Information Template

## Instructions
Replace the bracketed placeholders in `manuscript.tex` with your actual information:

## Current Placeholders in manuscript.tex:
```latex
\author{
    [Your Name]$^1$ \\
    \small $^1$[Your Institution], [Department], [City], [Country] \\
    \small Email: [your.email@institution.edu]
}
```

## Replace with your information:

### Example 1: Single Author
```latex
\author{
    John Smith$^1$ \\
    \small $^1$University of California, Department of Statistics, Berkeley, CA, USA \\
    \small Email: john.smith@berkeley.edu
}
```

### Example 2: Multiple Authors
```latex
\author{
    John Smith$^1$ and Jane Doe$^2$ \\
    \small $^1$University of California, Department of Statistics, Berkeley, CA, USA \\
    \small $^2$Stanford University, Department of Computer Science, Stanford, CA, USA \\
    \small Email: john.smith@berkeley.edu, jane.doe@stanford.edu
}
```

### Example 3: Academic Format
```latex
\author{
    Dr. John Smith$^1$ \\
    \small $^1$Department of Mathematics and Statistics, University of California, Berkeley, CA 94720, USA \\
    \small Email: jsmith@berkeley.edu
}
```

## Common Formats:

### For Academic Papers:
- Use your institutional email
- Include full department name
- Use standard academic formatting

### For Industry/Research Labs:
- Use your professional email
- Include company/organization name
- Include location (city, country)

## After Updating:
1. Save the manuscript.tex file
2. Compile in Overleaf
3. Check that your information appears correctly in the PDF
